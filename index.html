Robotics, perception, and mobile manipulation

I work on making robots behave reliably in the kinds of environments where things are rarely clean, static, or perfectly modelled.

I’m especially interested in robust, safe mobile manipulation for construction and infrastructure maintenance: systems that can move, sense, and manipulate in cluttered, changing spaces without needing everything to be carefully staged in advance.

Some themes

  • Connecting perception, mapping, and planning so robots can act usefully in the real world, not just in carefully controlled demos.
  • Mobile manipulation in semi-structured environments: construction sites, industrial facilities, and infrastructure corridors.
  • Safety and robustness in the presence of uncertainty, disturbances, and imperfect sensors.
  • Making it easier to deploy robots for dull, dirty, and dangerous tasks humans shouldn’t have to do.
